From a music file to a real piano performance.

PianoMate AI combines a desktop conversion workflow with an external piano-playing device. Music is converted into playback data, then PianoMate physically actuates the keys of a real acoustic piano.

Built for removable, real-piano playback.

The current PianoMate prototype focuses on simple external placement, AI-assisted music conversion, and real acoustic piano sound.

Non-destructive setup

No permanent structural modification. The device is designed to sit externally on the piano and be removed when needed.

AI-assisted conversion

The desktop workflow supports MP4, MP3, WAV, and MIDI sources for preparation of PianoMate playback files.

Real acoustic sound

PianoMate physically plays the keys instead of reproducing piano sound through ordinary speakers.

Phone control

Playback and device operation are designed to be controlled through Bluetooth connectivity.

Removable design

The current product is intended to be placed on the piano by hand and removed when it is not in use.

Multiple use cases

Suitable for home entertainment, piano demonstrations, music education, events, and video content creation.

FAQ

Early answers for crowdfunding visitors.

Does PianoMate require permanent piano modification?

No. The current concept is external and removable, with no permanent structural alteration to the piano.

Does it play piano sound through a speaker?

No. The device physically presses the keys, so the music is produced by the real acoustic piano.

What source files can the converter use?

The current desktop workflow supports MP4, MP3, WAV, and MIDI sources, especially piano-focused material.

How heavy is the current device?

The current prototype weighs approximately 11 kg. This helps maintain stability during key actuation.
